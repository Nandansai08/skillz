# Promotion Packet

## Overview

Packet quality is decided 6+ months before packet season: the year-round evidence file, the early gap-analysis with your manager (gaps found two quarters out are fillable; found at packet time they're next cycle's news), and claims calibrated precisely in both directions — the inflated "led" gets found by the committee, and the modest "we" erases you just as fatally.

## When to Use

- A promotion cycle approaches (ideally: months before), or the year-round evidence habit needs installing.
- Writing the self-review/promo doc, or advising someone through theirs.

**When NOT to use:**
- The comp conversation — `salary-negotiation` step 6.
- External applications — `resume-tailoring` (same evidence, different framing).

## Prerequisites

- The next level's actual rubric/ladder — cases argue against a written standard; no published ladder means extracting your manager's articulation of the bar, in writing, FIRST.

## The Workflow

1. **Run the evidence file year-round — the step that decides everything:** a running doc where wins land WHEN THEY HAPPEN — date, what, impact with numbers, link (PR, doc, dashboard, the thank-you), rubric dimension it feeds. The `weekly-review` step 7 feed makes it a 2-minute weekly habit. The alternative — reconstructing a year in packet week — loses the numbers and the quieter half of the wins entirely.

2. **Gap-analyze against the rubric before writing anything:** per dimension — strong / thin / genuine gap. Have the conversation with your manager EARLY ("what would make technical-leadership undeniable?") because gaps identified two quarters out are fillable (volunteer for the cross-team design, take the incident-command rotation). The manager-alignment is half the game: a promotion your manager is surprised by is a promotion that isn't happening.

3. **Frame every claim as impact, not activity:** the ladder's question is "what changed because you operated at the next level?" — each claim runs: outcome + your specific role + evidence link. "Redesigned the deploy pipeline" (activity) becomes "Cut deploy time 45→8 min (link), unblocking the daily-release goal — I identified the bottleneck, designed the fix, and led two engineers through the migration." Reviewers here CAN check the links.

4. **Calibrate scope claims to the level's altitude, precisely in both directions:** levels are distinguished by scope and autonomy, not effort — the packet shows next-level scope ALREADY OPERATING ("set the direction others executed"). Claim your true role: the "led" that was "participated" gets found by the committee's cross-checks (someone in that room was there), and one inflated claim discounts the honest majority — while systematic "we" makes a committee conclude the team did it all. The modesty tax is real, especially for those trained to pay it: first person, accurate.

5. **Weave claims into a narrative, not a list:** the strongest packets read as an arc — "over this period I moved from [prior scope] to [next-level scope]: [3–4 flagship examples, rubric-mapped]" — committees read dozens and remember arcs, not bullet piles. Flagships get depth; the supporting cast one line each. Address the known weak dimension head-on with its counter-evidence — the pre-emption reads as self-awareness; the omission as blindness.

6. **Recruit the supporting voices deliberately:** endorsers briefed with specifics — "the committee will probe cross-team influence; the API negotiation with your team is my best evidence — could you speak to that specifically?" gets testimony that lands; the unbriefed version gets a paragraph of generic praise. Give endorsers the claims relevant to them.

7. **If the answer is no, convert it into a contract:** "which specific dimension, and what evidence would change the answer, by when?" — written, turned into next cycle's gap plan, manager co-owning it. A vague no repeated twice is also information (`technology-evaluation`'s exit-pricing instinct applied to your own trajectory — sometimes the fastest promotion is elsewhere, where `resume-tailoring` inherits this file wholesale).

## Common Rationalizations

| Excuse | Why it doesn't hold |
|---|---|
| "I'll gather the evidence when the cycle opens" | Packet-week archaeology loses the numbers (the dashboard since changed) and the quieter half of the year outright. The file's weekly two minutes is the whole difference between thin and undeniable. |
| "Good work speaks for itself — my manager sees it" | Managers see fragments and remember quarters. The surprise packet gets no gap-filling, no advocacy, and argues alone in a room you're not in. |
| "Listing everything I did shows my value" | Twenty activities signal current-level busyness; the committee's question is what CHANGED at next-level scope. Impact framing isn't polish — it's the actual rubric question. |
| "Saying 'I' feels arrogant — 'we' is more accurate anyway" | Systematic 'we' makes the committee conclude the team did it all — the modesty tax, paid most by those trained to pay it. Your precise role, first person, is accuracy, not arrogance. |
| "Round up 'participated' to 'led' — everyone inflates" | Someone in the calibration room was there, and one found inflation discounts every honest claim in the packet. Precision beats inflation on pure expected value. |
| "Don't mention the weak dimension — why draw attention" | The committee finds it regardless; the omission reads as blindness where the pre-emption reads as self-awareness. Address it with counter-evidence or watch it addressed without you. |

## Red Flags

- No evidence file; packet season starting from memory.
- The manager learning of the ambition at packet time.
- Claims without links or numbers.
- A packet that maps to no rubric dimension anywhere.
- Endorsers asked for "feedback" with no specific claims to corroborate.
- A no accepted with no debrief contract; the same packet next cycle.

## Verification

- [ ] Evidence file active with dated, linked, rubric-tagged entries — ≥2 entries/month average.
- [ ] Gap analysis done with the manager ≥2 quarters before the cycle; fill-plan for thin dimensions in motion — notes exist.
- [ ] Every packet claim: outcome + role + link — spot-check five links resolve.
- [ ] Role calibration reviewed by someone who was there — no inflation, no erasure.
- [ ] Narrative arc present; weak dimension addressed with counter-evidence.
- [ ] Endorsers briefed with specific claims — messages sent.
- [ ] If declined: the written contract (dimension, evidence, date) exists with manager co-ownership.

## Example

Senior engineer targeting staff, six months out. Rubric gap-analysis with manager: technical execution strong, cross-team influence THIN, technical direction genuine gap. The two quarters bought by asking early: volunteered to lead the cross-team API-versioning effort (filling influence) and wrote the team's first architecture-direction doc (filling direction) — both landing in the evidence file alongside 30 weekly-review-fed entries. Packet written in two evenings from the file (versus the peer whose reconstruction took two weekends and still lacked numbers): arc "execution → org-level leverage," three flagships — the versioning effort (impact: unblocked two teams' roadmaps, link to the adopted RFC; role: "I drove the negotiation between three teams' conflicting constraints"), the direction doc (adopted, linked), and an incident-command example addressing the known weak spot head-on. Endorsers briefed with specific claims; the partner-team lead's feedback spoke directly to the negotiation. Committee outcome: promoted; the debrief noted the influence evidence as decisive — the dimension that had been THIN six months earlier, fillable only because it was found early.
