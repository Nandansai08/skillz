# Code of Conduct

## Our standard

This project welcomes contributions from everyone. We expect participants to:

- Be respectful in issues, PRs, and reviews. Critique skills, not people.
- Give review feedback the way the skills in this repo teach it: specific, evidence-based, severity-honest, and actionable.
- Assume good faith. A skill that misses the quality bar gets concrete revision guidance, not dismissal.
- Keep discussions on-topic and professional.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, personal attacks, or discriminatory language of any kind.
- Trolling, deliberate disruption, or sustained bad-faith argument.
- Publishing others' private information.
- Submitting skills designed to facilitate harm (e.g., malware development, harassment campaigns, academic dishonesty). Skills teaching security practices must be defensive or clearly scoped to authorized use.

## Enforcement

Violations can be reported by opening an issue marked `conduct` or contacting the maintainers directly. Maintainers will review and respond as promptly as practical, and may edit, remove, or reject contributions and comments, or ban contributors for behavior they deem inappropriate.
